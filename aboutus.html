Our Story

When Julius Sudi bought the first acre of land in Muranga in what he envisioned as Sudi Farms today in 1989 , he planted an avocado tree which still stands today as a result of careful tending to by all the members of the Sudi Family. The same nourishing hands still oversee and are involved in the farm to this day. We pride ourselves on supplying local markets supermarkets, butcheries, hotels, and institutions of knowledge, butcheries and individuals who buy in bulk.

Core Business

Sudi Farms' core strength is its large-scale farming operations, which enables us to achieve better quality and service levels. Today, Sudi has a land bank of 850 hectares in Kenya across three separate farms in Muranga with 80% of Sudi raw material being grown on Sudi farms.
